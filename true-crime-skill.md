# True Crime Faceless Scripts

> **OUTPUT MODE NOTE:** Visual cues like `[CLIP:]` and `[B-ROLL:]` shown here are for **Traditional mode**. For **Vidrush mode**, use the same structures but embed visual keywords naturally in prose instead. See master file for details.

## The True Crime Formula

True crime content follows a specific pattern that keeps viewers hooked through mystery, investigation, and resolution (or lack thereof).

---

## CRITICAL: Write Conversationally

**Your hooks should sound like someone telling a story, not a copywriter writing ad copy.**

Before using any hook, ask: "Would I actually say this out loud to a friend?"

If not, rewrite it to flow more naturally. Choppy sentence fragments ("10,000 feet. Freezing rain. Stakes.") sound written. Flowing narrative sounds spoken.

---

## HOOK TYPES FOR TRUE CRIME

### Type 1: The Conversational Mystery Hook (RECOMMENDED)

**Pattern:** Tell the story naturally + Stack the mystery + End on what we don't know

```
In 1971, a man walked onto a plane, handed a flight attendant a note, and
told her there was a bomb in his briefcase.

Four hours later, he jumped out the back with $200,000 in cash... into a
freezing thunderstorm... and was never seen again.

The FBI spent 45 years hunting him. They interviewed thousands of suspects,
tested DNA, chased every lead. And to this day, nobody knows who he was,
whether he survived, or where the money went.
```

**Why it works:**
- Flows like someone telling you a story at a bar
- No choppy fragments or "copywriter voice"
- Stakes build naturally through the narrative
- Ends on triple mystery (identity, survival, money)
- Could literally be spoken word-for-word

**When to use:** Heist stories, unsolved cases, disappearances, any mystery content

---

### Type 2: The Legendary Outsmarting Hook (Listicle Format)

**Pattern:** Flip expectations + "What about those who..."

```
We all have seen movies and cases of cops and legendary policemen who go
out of their way to catch criminals and end up arresting masterminds and
serial killers because of their wits. But what about the people who have
outwitted the police itself? Who have dodged the justice system as we
know it?

These are some of the legends that outwitted the police and actually got
away with it.
```

**When to use:** Listicle format where you're setting up multiple escape stories

---

### Type 2: The Historical Tragedy Hook

**Pattern:** Date + Scale + "This is the story of..."

```
On [DATE], [LOCATION] was forever changed when [BRIEF DESCRIPTION OF EVENT].
[SCALE OF IMPACT - deaths, damage, affected people].
This is the story of [EVENT NAME].
```

**When to use:** Mass events, terrorist attacks, disasters with criminal elements

---

### Type 3: The Victim Discovery Hook

**Pattern:** Normal situation + Discovery + Horrifying detail

```
[PERSON] was [doing normal activity] when [discovery happened].
[Horrifying specific detail about what was found].
What followed would [tease the investigation/outcome].
```

**When to use:** Murder cases, missing persons, crime scene stories

---

### Type 4: The Mastermind Reveal Hook

**Pattern:** Not who you think + Real mastermind + The plan

```
It was not [OBVIOUS SUSPECT] who was behind [CRIME].
In fact, it was [REAL MASTERMIND], [their background].
The plan was [years] in the making. And it almost worked.
```

**When to use:** Conspiracy cases, organized crime, elaborate schemes

## TRUE CRIME STRUCTURE (15-25 min)

### COLD OPEN (0:00-1:00)
The most dramatic moment from the middle or end of the story.

```
[VISUAL: Crime scene footage or recreation]
[DATE] - [LOCATION]

[Dramatic moment - the arrest, the discovery, the escape]

[TEXT ON SCREEN: "X days/months/years earlier"]

But to understand how we got here, we need to go back to the beginning.
```

**Purpose:** Create massive curiosity gap. Viewer needs to know how we got to this moment.

---

### THE SETUP (1:00-4:00)
Establish the normal world before everything changed.

**Include:**
- Who is the victim/criminal?
- What was their life like before?
- Any warning signs or foreshadowing?
- The world they existed in

```
[SUBJECT] was [their normal life].
[Specific details that humanize them].
[What made them vulnerable OR what led them to crime].

[Foreshadowing line]: But [SUBJECT] had no idea what was coming.
```

---

### THE CRIME (4:00-10:00)
Chronological account of what happened.

**Structure each phase:**
1. The approach/planning
2. The execution
3. The immediate aftermath
4. The discovery

```
[B-ROLL: Location footage]

On [DATE], at approximately [TIME], [what happened].

[Specific details - times, locations, witness accounts]

[CLIP: News footage if available]

[Build tension with each paragraph]

But [SUBJECT] made one critical mistake...
```

**Pacing:**
- New information every 15-30 seconds
- Rehook every 60-90 seconds
- Visual change every 3-5 seconds

---

### THE INVESTIGATION (10:00-16:00)
The hunt for answers.

**Include:**
- Initial police response
- Evidence gathered
- Dead ends and false leads
- Key breakthroughs (or why they never came)
- Suspects considered

```
When police arrived at the scene, they found [initial evidence].

Detective [NAME] was assigned to the case.

[CLIP: Press conference or interview footage]

The first suspect was [PERSON], because [reason].
But [why they were cleared OR why suspicion remained].

Then came the breakthrough that changed everything...
```

**For unsolved cases:**
```
Despite [efforts made], investigators hit dead end after dead end.
[Specific detail about what was tried].
The case went cold.

But years later, [new development OR why it remains unsolved].
```

---

### THE RESOLUTION (16:00-22:00)
Justice served (or not).

**For solved cases:**
```
On [DATE], [how the criminal was caught].

[The arrest/capture in detail]

[CLIP: Court footage or mugshot]

During the trial, [key revelations].

[Sentence/outcome]

[Victim impact - how families responded]
```

**For unsolved cases:**
```
To this day, [CASE] remains unsolved.

[Current status of investigation]

[Theories that exist]

The families of [VICTIMS] still wait for answers.

[Any recent developments or hope for resolution]
```

---

### THE AFTERMATH (22:00-24:00)
What happened after, and what we learned.

```
[Where is the criminal now?]

[What happened to the families?]

[Any policy changes or lasting impact?]

[Reflective statement about what this case teaches us]
```

---

### CTA (Final 30-60 seconds)

```
If you found this case as disturbing as I did, you might want to see
[related case video on screen].

And if you have any information about [unsolved case], contact
[authority].

Subscribe for more cases like this one.
```

## TENSION-BUILDING TECHNIQUES

### The "Almost Caught" Moment
```
Police were just [distance/time] away from [CRIMINAL].
If [small change], the entire case would have been different.
But [what actually happened].
```

### The Evidence Tease
```
There was one piece of evidence that investigators almost missed.
[Build anticipation]
[Reveal what it was]
This single [item] would change everything.
```

### The Victim Humanization
```
[VICTIM] was just [age] years old.
They had [dreams, family, plans].
[Specific detail that makes them real]
They had no idea that [foreshadowing].
```

### The Criminal Psychology
```
What drove [CRIMINAL] to [crime]?
According to [expert/profiler], [psychological insight].
But nothing could have prepared anyone for [escalation].
```

## REHOOKS FOR TRUE CRIME

**After evidence reveals:**
- "But this was just the beginning of what investigators would find..."
- "And that wasn't even the most disturbing part..."

**Before major turns:**
- "But then, everything changed."
- "What happened next shocked even the most seasoned detectives."

**For tension building:**
- "If police had arrived just five minutes earlier..."
- "The answer was right in front of them the whole time."

**For unsolved mysteries:**
- "To this day, no one knows..."
- "The truth may never be known."

---

## SENSITIVITY GUIDELINES

True crime requires balance between engagement and respect:

1. **Name victims, humanize them** - They're not just "the body"
2. **Don't glorify criminals** - Focus on impact, not infamy
3. **Be accurate** - Stick to verified facts
4. **Acknowledge ongoing pain** - Families are still affected
5. **Avoid graphic details** - Suggest, don't describe violence explicitly
