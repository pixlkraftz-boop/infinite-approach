# Title Formulas Skill

Proven title frameworks for faceless YouTube content. These work for narration-over-footage formats: documentary, exposé, true crime, celebrity, business, history, and explainers.

**How to use:** Replace X, Y, Z with your topic. Keep the structure intact.

## TITLE WRITING TIPS

### What Makes Titles Click

1. **Specificity beats vague** - "How He Lost $47 Million" beats "How He Lost Money"
2. **Numbers add credibility** - Use real figures when possible
3. **Negativity outperforms positivity** - Downfall > Success (for CTR)
4. **Open loops** - "Here's Why…" makes viewers need to click
5. **Authority adds weight** - "What Lawyers Don't Tell You" beats "What People Don't Tell You"

### Match Title to Thumbnail

Your title and thumbnail must work together:
- Title: The text hook (curiosity, stakes, promise)
- Thumbnail: The visual hook (emotion, subject, intrigue)

Don't repeat the same information in both. They should complement, not duplicate.

### Avoid These Mistakes

- **Too long** - Keep under 60 characters when possible
- **Clickbait without payoff** - Delivers what the title promises
- **Vague subjects** - "This Person" loses to actual names
- **No emotional hook** - Every title needs stakes
